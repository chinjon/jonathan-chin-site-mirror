i still don't know what to do with myself, two weeks after the fact. the knicks did it. the knicks were the last one standing. the knicks did the thing that many of us feared would never happen in our lifetime. they won. watching game five felt like a blur. 95% of the time i thought they were going back to the garden, although based on the past 2 months, i really shouldn't have. after the final 3 point hail mary from wemby and the rebound/victory hurl from og, i screamed.

the day after, i was at a gas station filling up my car, and the thought hit me, what about the next one. can they repeat? seasons after this one won't feel as anguished, no more "_well maybe next year_" or "_lets see what they do in the offseason_". it was almost a habit, or a fact of life, the knicks falling short, not a matter of if but by how much. 

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uWa2Kcxi-l0

in the ever present glow and unfamiliar aura, i have been thinking about what it took to get here. does the constant fumbling and maddening mismanagement of everything make it feel much more sweeter that it would with a team that was consistently at the vestibule of a championship? the point guards that never were, the just-okay rosters, the bad luck draft lottos, the false hopes, i could go on. ron baker, lou amundson, david lee, damyean dotson, langston galloway, chris duhon, alonzo trer, cleanthony early, frank ntilikina, kristaps porzingis, etcetera. but in spite of that, some of us remained loyal, still selling out games even during their worst seasons, as if there was no other team in the league. _its new york or no one_.

<img class="blog-image" src="https://encrypted-tbn0.gstatic.com/images?q=tbn:ANd9GcQUagIrQpSNsqsDf700UTNGFDTSh5Mi1448OP364eYTTyYgwa4QqULsUm9K&s=10))" alt="jalen brunson and patrick ewing holding the finals mvp and larry o'brien trophy, respectively" width="300">

the image the solidified the championship in my mind is the photo of patrick ewing posing with jalen. patrick with the larry o'brien trophy, jalen with the finals mvp trophy, both smiling. to me, theres a sense of relief that coursed through patrick, but a sense of child-like joy exuding from jalen. this image alludes to a lot of the celebration, one that cuts across generations, one that, just maybe, would tide over for a lifetime.
