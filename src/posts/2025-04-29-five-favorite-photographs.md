i wanted to try out this exercise of documenting and writing about my favorite photos. not necessarily the "all-time" favorites, like with "favorites" in other hobbies and cultural interests, i think they're something of a reflection of how we currently see the world or even ourselves. life isn't static so our "favorites" should change as well.

_all photos credit to their respective photographers and estates_

***

**bruce davidson - katz's deli (2004)**  

![bruce-davidson-katz](https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/jonathankchin/bruce-davidson-katz.webp)

bruce davidson is known for his hyper-focused documentary work, often dedicating his work to a theme or idea. his most well-known works have been his documentation of the the nyc subway system and [photographing a single street in harlem](https://www.magnumphotos.com/arts-culture/society-arts-culture/bruce-davidson-east-100th-street/). but my personal favorite is when he photographed patrons of katz's deli for an assignment. i believe that this was for an assignment where he was given a choice of what to photograph, and  he immediately thought of katz's deli, a new york institution. using an ultra-wide lens, i believe a 24mm, he gets close with an off-body flash, adding to the whimsy and the very crowded seating arrangements of Katz's. the whole series reeks of quintessential new york.

***

**eugene richards - under the manhattan bridge - brooklyn, ny (1993)**

![eugene-richards-dumbo](https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/jonathankchin/eugene-richards-dumbo.webp)

speaking of "all-time favorites", eugene richards is one for me. he mixes documentary photography ethos with the framing of a painter, i have always put him up there with the best of them. i always refer to him as "a poet with a camera". this particular photo resonates with me a lot, taken in brooklyn down under the manhattan bridge on a hot summer day. richards came across the scene after finishing up an assignment at gleason's gym and was on his way home. to me, this photo is one of the images i think about when i think about quintessential new york, a family, improvising their way into experiencing a simple pleasure in life. 

***

**mary ellen mark - laurie in the ward 81 tub, oregon state hospital (1976)**

![Mary_Ellen_Mark_ward-81](https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/jonathankchin/mary_ellen_mark_ward-81.webp)

i don't think there is a need for me to gush about mary ellen mark, as anyone familiar with her photos, they speaks for humanity and life. working on a self-assigned project, mark had wanted to document the lives of the women that lived in the high-security, all-female wing of the oregon state hospital, along with sociologist dr. karen folger jacobs. for an entire month, mark and dr. jacobs lived with [the women of ward 81](https://cpw.org/exhibition/exhibitions-mary-ellen-mark-ward-81/), allowing mark to document the women, giving them the dignity and narrative that many living on the margins of society are not granted to given the chance to have.

***

**chien-chi chang - a newly arrived immigrant eating noodles on a fire escape. new york city (1998)** 

![FwLYkANWwAAwssL](https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/jonathankchin/fwlykanwwaawssl.webp)

chien-chi chang is a taiwanese photographer who is known for his work documenting the life of immigrants and the promise and trepidation that new beginnings come with. his on-going project documenting new york city chinatown has resonated with me personally, in which he [described the neighborhood as new york's "capital city of promise"](https://www.magnumphotos.com/arts-culture/society-arts-culture/chien-chi-chang-chinatown/). throughout the 90's, chang made photos of undocumented immigrants living in chinatown, many of them from the fujian province in china. the series often shows these immigrants disconnected and alienated from the bustle of the city, some of the alienation is attributed to the long hours worked to send back any portion of their lowly pay to their families in china. this particular resonated with me, which always reminds me of my mother and grandparents (maternal and paternal), thinking of how their lives were upon arrival to new york city's chinatown.

---

**garry winogrand -  elliot richardson press conference, austin 1973** 

![garry-winogrand-public-relations](https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/jonathankchin/garry-winogrand-public-relations.webp)

while garry winogrand is known for his prolific street photography, my favorite project is his work "public relations", where he wanted to examine "the effect of media on events”. this particular photo is of former us attorney general, elliot richardson holding a press conference at the university of austin texas. rather than focusing on richarson himself, winogrand captured the spectacle that followed him. for me, i've been fascinated with the celebrity culture and how we as a society not only enable the spectacle, but inject ourselves in the spectacle with the act of documentation. i always think about how this photo would be the same if you replace the tape recorders with cell phones. on a side note, i recommend reading [neil postman's "amusing ourselves to death"](https://slate.com/culture/2025/03/amusing-ourselves-death-neil-postman-ezra-klein.html).
