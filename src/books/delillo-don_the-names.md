decided to go with this one as my second delillo, as per a recommendation from some one at the bookstore, who claimed that this was delillo's best. this is the novel that marked a transition phase for delillo, moving to greece and laying the groundwork for his more well-known post-modern works, where he begins to go deeper into ideas of america what it means to be an american. at first it felt like a thriller in some ways (ie: the murderous cult), the progression is a little sticky at points, but evolved into a surreal kind of read. didn't really understand the book until 5-10 minutes after i finished it, it left me in hazy chill for the next few days. there is a bit on francis ford coppola that's pretty funny.
